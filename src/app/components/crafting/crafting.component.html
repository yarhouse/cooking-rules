Manufacturing

Manufacturing is the process of using tools and raw materials to craft non-magical items. Choose a tool you are proficient with, gather the required materials, and make a check (tool ability modifier + proficiency). Meeting or exceeding the item's DC produces the item in the listed time. Failing by 4 or less wastes only your time; failing by 5 or more also destroys half the materials.

Key Rules

  • You must be proficient with the relevant tool.
  • Each item lists a material cost, DC, and crafting time.
  • Up to 3 proficient helpers can assist — each adds their proficiency bonus.
  • Helpers also reduce the time (divide by number of participants).
  • You can hire a journeyman (×1 speed, +6 mod, 1 gp/hr) or expert (×2 speed, +8 mod, 4 gp/hr).

Enchanting

Enchanting imbues an existing item with magic using the essence of a slain creature. Make an enchanting check using the skill associated with the creature type whose essence you are using. You must have the required essence and have it in your possession for the full crafting time.

Key Rules

  • Each magic item specifies the required creature type and component.
  • The check uses INT, WIS, or CHA (your choice) + proficiency in the associated skill.
  • Essence is consumed on success or failure.
  • Enchanting boons accumulate based on the essence rarity used (max 3).
  • Enchanting quirks do not stack — each unique quirk can only apply once.

Monster Forging

Monster forging uses harvested creature parts — hide, bone, antlers, talons, and similar components — in place of standard materials when crafting armour and weapons. The creature must be at least the minimum size listed for each item type. Monster-forged items use the same DC and time as normal crafted versions but may grant additional properties based on the creature's abilities (GM discretion).
