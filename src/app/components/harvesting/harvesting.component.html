How Harvesting Works

Harvesting is the act of extracting magical components from a slain creature before its latent magic fades. You have five steps — and a tight time limit.

1
Description The GM consults the creature's type to determine which components are available. Boss monsters have unique harvest tables. CR also determines which essence tier can be extracted.
2
Harvest List The party decides what to harvest and in what order. Order matters — components later in the list require higher cumulative DCs to reach.
3
Harvest DCs Each component has a Component DC. The Harvest DC for each item is the cumulative sum of all Component DCs above it in the list. Example: Eyes (DC 5) → Pouch of Teeth (DC 10+5=15) → Hide (DC 20+15=35).
4
Harvesting Check Two harvesters make separate checks — an Assessing Harvester (Intelligence + skill) and a Carving Harvester (Dexterity + skill, or spellcasting ability for ritual carving). One creature can do both but makes both checks at disadvantage.
5
Loot Add the two check results together. You successfully harvest every component whose Harvest DC is met or exceeded. Components above your total are lost.

Degradation

  • A harvester must begin within 1 minute of the creature's death.
  • Harvesting must not be interrupted once started.
  • A character can only fully harvest one creature per battle.
  • Gentle repose does not prevent degradation.

Helpers

Additional creatures can assist by adding their proficiency bonus (or half, rounded down, without proficiency) to the check result. Helpers must assist for the entire duration.

Ritual Carving

When harvesting Aberrations, Celestials, Elementals, Fey, or Fiends, a carving harvester with a spellcasting ability can substitute their spellcasting modifier for Dexterity.

Volatile Components

Components marked warning can explode if you start harvesting them but don't finish. The GM uses the creature's ability to determine the save DC and effect.
